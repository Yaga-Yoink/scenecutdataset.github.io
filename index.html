Abstract

We introduce a new benchmark dataset for evaluating object separation in 3D reconstruction. Object separation in 3D is fairly new topic and lacks the proper benchmark dataset. Previous methods evaluated their approach on cropped sub-meshes from a full scene, which has holes in occluded regions. Thus, during the evaluation, the area that needs to be properly evaluated will be ignored. To address the gap in the literature, we introduce a new benchmarking dataset composed of real-world and synthetic scenes.
